# Template 03 — Use Case A (Lifestyle / Context Image)

**Reference Image:** `03_Use_Case_1.png`
**Carousel Slot:** Typically Image 4 or 5. By this point the customer understands what the product is — now they need to understand where it fits in their actual life.

---

## What This Image Must Do

Use Case A answers the question: "When do I use this, and where does it work on my body?" It's a context image. It takes the product out of the abstract and puts it into a real situation — the heel, the toe, the arch, the hand — so the customer can visualize themselves using it.

This template is especially effective for products that cover a body zone (socks, sleeves, bandages, gloves) because it can show multiple placement scenarios in a grid format: here's where it goes on the heel, here's the toe, here's the arch. Each zone is labeled, annotated, and sized. The customer leaves this image understanding exactly what they're buying, where it goes, and that it was designed to fit their specific anatomy.

There is often a lifestyle photo component — a real person using the product in context, not just a cutout — which adds warmth and credibility.

---

## Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 140 | Two-tone color split. Smaller than Feature Dominant headers because the image grid takes more visual space. |
| Sub | Poppins Medium | 56 | Supporting line below the header. Frames the use case conceptually — e.g., "Our variety pack has a bandage for every blister." |
| Descriptor | Poppins Medium | 50 | Labels for each zone or scenario in the grid — e.g., "Heel", "Toe", "Anywhere that needs love". Short, direct. |
| Measurement | Poppins Bold | 25 | Physical dimensions shown inline — e.g., "1.13" × 3.75"", "2.38" × 0.6"". These appear beneath or beside the product/zone image. |

### Header Two-Tone Rule
At size 140, the two-tone format still applies. The header in the reference image reads "SELF CARE" (dark neutral) + "IN 3 SIZES" (product accent). The structure names the category, then delivers the specific claim.

---

## Layout Structure

This template has two sub-variants within the same layout logic:

**Sub-Variant 1: Grid with lifestyle photo**
- Header + sparkles appear in the upper left, inside a colored block (product accent fill).
- To the right of the header block, a lifestyle/in-use photo occupies the upper right — showing the product on a real body (foot, hand, etc.). This is a cropped close-up, not a full-body shot.
- Below the header block, a 2×2 grid (or similar) of body zone photos fills the lower half. Each grid cell shows a different placement scenario with a Descriptor label and an arrow or pointer line.
- Measurement callouts ("1.13" × 3.75"") appear at the bottom of each grid cell, indicating the actual physical size of the product shown.

**Sub-Variant 2: Dark background version**
- Same structure, but the header block switches from a light product accent to deep navy (`#1d396a`). The Neuzeit header text becomes cream or white. The grid photos and annotations remain the same.
- This variant gives a premium, editorial feel vs. the lighter variant's more approachable feel.

**Pointer/Bracket Annotation System**
- Curved arrows (in the product accent color) point from the Descriptor label to the specific body zone in the photo.
- Arrows should feel hand-indicated — not mechanical. They direct attention without being clinical.
- Measurement dimensions are shown near the bottom of the relevant zone, paired with or below the arrow annotation.

## Design Elements Required

| Element | Placement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 3 Sparkles (2-color or 3-color) | Inside or adjacent to the header block | 3-color variant is also acceptable here — Use Case A is one of the only templates where the 3-color sparkle appears. |
| Curved Annotation Arrows | Within each grid cell, pointing to body zone | Color matches product accent. Creates a hand-annotated feel. |
| Measurement Callouts | Below each grid cell, near the relevant zone | Poppins Bold 25. Show actual dimensions (inches or centimeters). |
| Lifestyle Photo | Upper right zone | Real in-use shot — product on body, natural lighting. Not a studio cutout. |
| Grid Zone Photos | Lower half | Close-up of each body zone with product applied. Clean, clinical-but-warm photography. |

---

## Color Guidance

- **Header block fill:** Product accent color (saturated) or deep navy. The block behind the header text is the loudest color on the canvas — it anchors the composition.
- **Header text:** Cream (`#fef5e3`) or white when the block is dark; dark neutral when the block is light.
- **Two-tone split inside header:** If the header block is light, use dark neutral + accent. If block is dark/navy, both words can sit in cream/white — the block itself provides the color contrast.
- **Grid cell backgrounds:** White or very light tint. They should feel clinical and clean — the product photos carry the visual weight here.
- **Annotation arrows:** Product accent color — same shade used in the header emphasis word.
- **Measurement text:** Dark neutral or navy. Never the accent color — measurements need to read as technical information, not design flourish.

## What Good Looks Like

In `03_Use_Case_1.png`, the layout shows two colorway variants side by side — one with a light sky-blue header block, one with deep navy. Both use the same 2×2 grid below showing Heel (upper right), Toe (lower left), and "Anywhere that needs love" (lower right). The curved arrows in each cell do real informational work — they connect the label text to the body zone being annotated. The measurement dimensions at the bottom of each cell ("1.13" × 3.75"", "2.38" × 0.6"") give the customer precise sizing data without making the image feel like a spec sheet.

The lifestyle photo (foot with blue-polished toes) provides warmth and grounds the clinical grid in a real human experience.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Grid cells without measurements.** If you're showing multiple sizes or placement zones, each needs a dimension. It's a key purchase driver for fit-sensitive products.
- **Arrows that point at nothing specific.** The arrow should land on the exact body zone — heel, toe, pad center. Vague arrow placement undermines the annotation system.
- **Too many grid cells.** 4 cells (2×2) is the maximum before the image gets cluttered at mobile size. If you have more than 4 scenarios, consider a second Use Case image.
- **Lifestyle photo missing.** The grid alone is purely functional. The lifestyle photo adds the human warmth that makes this template convert.
- **Sparkle absent.** Even with a text-heavy layout, the 3-sparkle brand graphic must appear near the header. It signals brand identity.
- **Header block same color as grid backgrounds.** The header block needs to stand out strongly from the grid. If both are light, use a border or increase the contrast.
