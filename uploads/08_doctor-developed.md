# Template 08 — Doctor Developed

**Reference Image:** `08_Doctor_Developed.png`
**Carousel Slot:** Can appear almost anywhere in the carousel — often Image 3 or 7. It's a trust pivot: it interrupts the product story to say "someone credible stands behind this."

---

## What This Image Must Do

The Doctor Developed template is a credibility image. It establishes that Dr. Frederick's Original is not a generic brand reselling manufactured products — it is a physician-designed line, backed by a real doctor with real credentials, who designed these products for real patients.

In a market full of "as seen on TV" products and dubious wellness brands, physician endorsement is a meaningful differentiator. This image makes that differentiator impossible to miss. The doctor's portrait, signature, and credentials are the primary visual elements. The product may appear, but it's secondary to the human authority figure.

The tone must be warm and aspirational, not clinical. This is not a medical disclaimer. It's a personal message from a doctor who genuinely cares about the customer's wellbeing. The copy should sound like something a trusted physician would say — encouraging, specific, and human.

## Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 200 | Two-tone color format. At 200, this is one of the largest headers in the system. Short — 2–4 words. |
| Features | Poppins SemiBold | 50 | Body copy beneath the header — the aspirational paragraph. Also used for benefit callout lines if present. |

### Header Two-Tone Rule
At 200, two-tone is required. The reference image shows "DO MORE" (dark neutral) + "OF WHAT" (transition) + "YOU LOVE" (accent color) — or collapsed to a 2-line structure with the accent on the emotional peak. The header should be aspirational and direct: it's addressed to the customer's life, not to the product's features.

### The Feature / Body Copy
The Poppins SemiBold 50 body copy beneath the header is the doctor's voice. It should be 3–5 sentences, written in first person or as a direct address. It references the product specifically but leads with the customer's experience: "Keep going, explore more, and reach new heights. With the Better Blister Bandages, designed to comfort and heal, we're here to support every step you take."

This is not a feature list. This is a relationship statement.

---

## Layout Structure

The Doctor Developed template is the simplest structural layout in the system. It's essentially two columns: copy and portrait.

**Left Column — Copy + Header + Signature**
- The header sits at the top of the left column. Large. Two-tone. Bold claim about the customer's life.
- Below it, the body copy (Poppins SemiBold 50) fills 3–5 sentences.
- At the bottom of the copy block: the authority signature element.
  - A handwritten-style signature of the doctor's name — "Dr. Frederick" in a script font or genuine handwritten style, prefixed with a dash (— Dr. Frederick).
  - Below the signature: the doctor's full name and credentials in Poppins SemiBold — "Ben Frederick, MD".
  - The signature block is the emotional peak of this template. It personalizes the brand in a way no icon or badge can.

**Right Column — Doctor Portrait**
- A professional photo of the doctor. Well-lit. Arms crossed or in a warm, confident pose. White lab coat.
- The portrait is large — at least half the canvas height. It should feel like you're looking at a real person, not a stock photo.
- The portrait photo extends close to the edge of the canvas, creating a sense of presence.

**Sparkle Accent**
- One or two sparkles (from the brand graphic system) appear near the header — typically at the top right of the header text block or above the header. Small, decorative, present. They connect this image to the brand graphic system without overwhelming the credibility tone.

**Dark and Light Background Variants**
This template has two color variants in the reference image:
- **Light variant:** White or very light background. The portrait and copy sit cleanly against it. Feels approachable and warm.
- **Dark variant:** Deep navy or soft sky-blue background. The copy in cream, the portrait with a slight background block. Feels more premium and authoritative.

Both variants work. Choose based on the product's color system — the dark variant pairs well with products that use navy or teal as their primary accent.

## Color Guidance

- **Light variant background:** White or warm cream (`#fef5e3`). 
- **Dark variant background:** Deep navy (`#1d396a`) or soft sky blue (`#a8cae4`).
- **Header base word(s):** Near-black or navy on light background; cream on dark background.
- **Header accent word:** Product accent color on both backgrounds. On dark backgrounds, the accent may be lighter/brighter to maintain contrast.
- **Body copy:** Dark neutral (`#231f20`) on light background; cream (`#fef5e3`) on dark background.
- **Signature script:** Dark neutral on light; cream on dark.
- **Credentials line:** Same as body copy color.
- **Sparkles:** Product accent color. If the image background is dark, sparkles can be cream or white to read cleanly.

---

## The Authority Signature Element — Detailed Rules

The signature is the most delicate element in this template. It must look authentic, not designed.

- The script portion should be a genuine handwritten-style signature or an actual scan of Dr. Frederick's signature. If generating with AI, specify: "handwritten cursive signature, not calligraphy, casual professional style."
- Prefix with "—" (em dash + space). The dash reads as a personal attribution.
- Full name and credentials beneath in printed type: "Ben Frederick, MD" — Poppins SemiBold, smaller than the body copy.
- The gap between the script signature and the printed name should be consistent — it should feel like a business card or a letter closing.
- Never use a bold decorative font as the "signature." It destroys authenticity.

## What Good Looks Like

In `08_Doctor_Developed.png`, both the light and dark variants show a two-column layout with remarkable visual simplicity. The header "DO MORE OF WHAT YOU LOVE" fills the upper left in a 3-line two-tone stack. The body copy in Poppins SemiBold 50 fills the middle — 3 sentences, warm and direct. The signature block appears at the bottom of the copy column with the script name and "Ben Frederick, MD" beneath. The doctor portrait fills the entire right column — confident, professional, and distinctly human.

The sparkle appears at approximately the 1 o'clock position relative to the last word of the header — small, present, brand-consistent.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Stock photo doctor.** The portrait must look like Dr. Frederick specifically, or a specific real doctor. A generic white coat image from a stock library reads as fake and undermines the entire purpose of this template.
- **Feature list in place of body copy.** This is the one template where bullet points and benefit callouts should not appear. The copy is a paragraph. A human voice. A relationship.
- **Signature that looks designed.** Overly decorative script kills authenticity. The signature should look like it was written with a Sharpie, not typeset.
- **No credentials.** "Ben Frederick, MD" must appear below the signature. Credentials are the whole point.
- **Header too long.** At 200pt, the header must fit in 2–3 lines at most. Cut to the emotional core.
- **Sparkle missing.** Even in this credibility-focused image, the sparkle connects it to the brand system. Without it, this could be any healthcare brand.
- **Both variants same background.** Use the dark variant when the product line has a dark/navy primary palette; use the light variant when the product line has a warm/cream/teal primary palette.
