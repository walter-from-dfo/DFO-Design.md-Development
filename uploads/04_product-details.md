# Template 04 — Product Details

**Reference Image:** `04_Product_Details.png`
**Carousel Slot:** Typically Image 5 or 6. The customer is past the hook — this image answers: "What exactly am I getting?"

---

## What This Image Must Do

Product Details communicates what's in the box, how many, how big, and what each piece is for. It eliminates the purchase anxiety that comes from not knowing what you'll receive. For products that come in variety packs, multi-size kits, or multi-piece sets, this template is essential — it turns "I hope it fits" into "I know exactly what I'm getting."

The dominant visual element here is a large number. The quantity — 10x, 3x, 6x — is the hero. It's rendered at size 120, which makes it the largest typographic element on the canvas. The product image and header support it, but the number does the primary persuasive work: it signals value, completeness, and specificity.

Think of this as the "unboxing slide" in the carousel. If the Feature Dominant image sold the dream, this image confirms the deal.

---

## Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 212 | The largest header in the entire template system. Two-tone format. Short — 2–4 words max. |
| Sub (Main Label) | Poppins Regular | 67 | Descriptive subtitle — the full product name or a "what you get" statement. Regular weight for contrast against the SemiBold accent. |
| Sub (Accent) | Poppins SemiBold | 67 | The emphasized portion of the subtitle — often a material name, count, or key product noun. Same size as Main Label but heavier weight. |
| Product Quantity | Poppins SemiBold | 120 | The DOMINANT typographic element. The number that communicates how much the customer gets — 10x, 3x, 6x, etc. This is visually larger than the header in some layouts. |
| Specific Use | Poppins SemiBold | 56 | Labels beside the quantity number — e.g., "HEEL", "SPOT", "TOE". Identifies what the quantity applies to. |
| Measurement | Poppins Bold | 40 | Physical dimensions overlaid on or beside the product image — e.g., "1.75"", "2.75"". |

### Header Two-Tone Rule
At size 212, the two-tone format is mandatory. In the reference image this reads "HAPPY" (dark neutral) + "HEALING" (product accent orange). The header names the outcome, not the product — it's aspirational even in a functional template.

### The Sub Pair Rule
The Main Label + Accent Sub lines work as a pair. They're the same size (67) but different weights. Together they read as a single statement with emphasis: "With **10 Hydrocolloid Blister Bandages**" — "With" in Regular, the product descriptor in SemiBold. This creates rhythm and draws the eye to the specific claim.

## Layout Structure

**Top Zone — Header**
- The two-tone header sits at the top of the canvas. Large. Commanding.
- Below it, the Sub pair (Main Label + Accent) provides the product description.

**Center Zone — Quantity + Product**
- The quantity number (size 120) is placed prominently in the center-left of the image, large enough to read at thumbnail size.
- The product image sits beside or behind the quantity number. The product image can be the packaging (box, bag, tube) or the product itself.
- Wave shape element curves beneath or behind the product image as a background layer. Colored to the product accent.
- Tab feature design element — a small tab-shaped accent — appears near the product as a subtle visual anchor. Use it to label a variant, color, or model identifier.

**Breakdown Zone — Multiple Quantities**
- If the product has multiple size variants or the kit contains multiple pieces, the layout expands into multiple quantity+label pairs.
- Each piece/size gets its own Quantity number + Specific Use label + Measurement callout.
- In the reference image, this looks like: "3x [SPOT] 1.13" × ..." + "3x [TOE] 0.6" × ..." + "6x [HEEL] 1.75" × ..." arranged in a grid with the product image at center.
- The 3-sparkle brand graphic appears in this zone — often near the breakdown numbers as a visual break between sets.

**Bottom Zone — Sparkles + Optional Footer**
- The 3-sparkle (2-color) graphic sits near the breakdown area or at the bottom of the product zone.
- An optional footer line can appear at the very bottom for a URL, offer, or tagline.

## Design Elements Required

| Element | Placement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Wave Shape | Behind or beneath the product image, center zone | Accent color. Creates a sense of depth and grounds the product. |
| Tab Feature Element | Adjacent to product image or near variant label | Small, subtle. A tab or flag shape that labels a key detail. |
| 3 Sparkles (2-color) | Near the breakdown numbers or product zone | Standard placement — upper right of product or beside a quantity number. |
| Measurement Callouts | Overlaid on or beside the product image | Poppins Bold 40. Point to specific zones with subtle leader lines. |

---

## Color Guidance

- **Background:** Warm cream (`#fef5e3`) or white. The background should feel like an unboxing surface — clean, premium, neutral.
- **Header base word:** Near-black (`#231f20`) or deep navy.
- **Header accent word:** Product line accent color.
- **Quantity numbers:** Dark neutral or product accent — large enough that they read at 375px width.
- **Specific Use labels:** Can be placed inside a small colored pill/tag for visual separation. Use the product accent or a complementary tone.
- **Wave shape:** Soft tint of the product accent — not full saturation. It's backdrop, not focal point.
- **Measurement callouts:** Dark neutral. Clinical precision; avoid colorizing these.

## What Good Looks Like

In `04_Product_Details.png`, the left variant shows "HAPPY HEALING / With 10 Hydrocolloid Blister Bandages" with the quantity "10x" rendered enormous against the product packaging. The measurement callouts (1.75" and 2.75") are overlaid directly on the image with leader lines pointing to the heel pad and the surrounding area. The right variant shows the same product in a 3-count breakdown: "3x SPOT" + "3x TOE" + "6x HEEL" — each with its own quantity number, use label, and measurements. The 3-sparkle graphic sits near the "6x HEEL" section.

The product packaging is the same in both variants; what changes is the quantity story being told.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Quantity number too small.** If the "10x" doesn't feel dominant, scale it up. It should be visually comparable to or larger than the product image.
- **Measurements missing.** This is a dimensions-and-details template. If there's a size, show it. Customers need to know before they buy.
- **Too many pieces in the breakdown.** If the kit has 12+ variants, group them (e.g., "3x Heel", "3x Toe", "6x Ball of Foot") rather than listing individually.
- **Wave shape absent.** Without it, the center zone feels flat. The wave adds depth and contextualizes the product.
- **Header too long.** At size 212, the header needs to be 2–4 words. "HAPPY HEALING" is perfect. "YOUR COMPLETE BLISTER HEALING KIT" is too long.
- **Sub pair in same weight.** If both sub lines are SemiBold or both Regular, they blur together. The Regular + SemiBold pairing creates the necessary rhythm.
