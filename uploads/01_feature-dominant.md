# Template 01 — Feature Dominant

**Reference Image:** `01_Feature_Dominant.png`
**Carousel Slot:** Typically Image 1 or 2. This is a lead image — it sets the visual tone for the entire set.

---

## What This Image Must Do

The Feature Dominant template has one job: make the product look so good the customer doesn't need to ask questions. The product is large, lit beautifully, and surrounded by benefit callouts that support it without competing with it. A customer scrolling at thumb speed should be able to read the header, absorb two or three benefits, and feel confident — all in under two seconds.

The word "dominant" is literal. The product takes up more real estate than anything else on the canvas. Typography plays a supporting role. Everything else — the wave shape, the sparkles, the color blocks — exists to frame the product, not distract from it.

---

## Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 194 | Two-tone color split. First word/phrase in dark neutral; emphasis word(s) in product accent color. |
| Feature Label | Poppins SemiBold | 52 | The short bold benefit name — e.g., "Blocks Bacteria & Water". Placed in a darker block for contrast. |
| Descriptor | Poppins Medium | 42 | Supporting line beneath the feature label. Adds context in a lighter weight — e.g., "Completely Moisture-Proof, Semi-Translucent". |
| Disclaimer / Caption | Poppins Medium | 24 | Fine print or secondary note at the bottom. Small but legible. |

### Header Two-Tone Rule
At size 194, the header must use the two-tone format. The first word or phrase sits in near-black (`#231f20`) or deep navy (`#1d396a`). The emphasis word — the word that carries the emotional punch — switches to the product's accent color. In the reference image this reads as "BLISTER" in navy, "BE GONE" in orange. The color break happens at the natural language emphasis point, not arbitrarily.

---

## Layout Structure

The canvas is divided into two vertical zones:

**Left Zone — Typography + Callouts**
- Header anchors the top left. It's the first thing the eye lands on.
- Below the header, benefit callouts stack vertically. Each callout is a pair: Feature Label on top (bold, in a dark contrast block), Descriptor beneath it (lighter weight, more detail).
- Callouts are left-aligned. There are typically 3 callouts — enough to build confidence, not so many they become a list.
- The wave shape element runs along the top or bottom of this zone as a background layer, pulling the product accent color.

**Right Zone — Product**
- The product image is large and centered in this zone.
- It gets a drop shadow to lift it off the background, especially critical when the product packaging color is close to the background tint.
- The product should be photographed cleanly — cutout or nearly so — so it reads as a premium object.
- Sparkle graphic (3-sparkle, 2-color) is placed near the product, either beside it or above it in the corner. Sparkle colors pull from the same accent palette as the header emphasis color.

---

## Design Elements Required

| Element | Placement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Wave Shape | Top or bottom of left zone, behind callouts | Color matches product accent. Creates depth without competing. |
| 3 Sparkles (2-color) | Near the product or upper area of the product zone | Both sparkle colors pull from the product accent palette. Larger sparkle in the primary accent, smaller two in lighter/secondary tones. |
| Drop Shadow | Applied to product image | Essential when product and background share similar tones. Shadow should be soft, not hard-edged. |
| Dark Contrast Block | Behind Feature Labels | A darker rectangle or pill shape behind each Feature Label. Prevents the bold text from floating on a light background. |

---

## Color Guidance

- **Background:** White, warm cream, or a very light tint of the product accent color. Never saturated. The background recedes; the product advances.
- **Header base word:** Near-black (`#231f20`) or deep navy (`#1d396a`).
- **Header accent word:** Product line's accent color — see color-system.md for per-SKU palettes.
- **Feature blocks:** Darker shade from the same accent family, or deep navy. Should contrast clearly against the background.
- **Wave shape:** Mid-tone of the product accent. Not full saturation — it's a texture element, not a focal point.
- **Sparkles:** Primary accent (larger sparkle) + lighter secondary tone (smaller sparkles).

## What Good Looks Like

Looking at the reference image (`01_Feature_Dominant.png`), you'll see multiple colorway variants side by side — one with warm orange/peach tones, one with navy/teal tones, one with the pink/rose gel sock. Each variant proves the template is palette-agnostic: the structure stays identical while the accent color changes completely. This is intentional. The template is a chassis; the product line paints it.

The product in every variant is large enough that you could identify it from across a room. The callouts are readable but never overwhelm the product.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Product too small.** If you're reading the callout text before you notice the product, the product needs to be larger.
- **Callouts too many.** More than 4 callouts starts to feel like a spec sheet. Cut to the 3 most differentiating benefits.
- **Accent color used too liberally.** The accent is for the header emphasis word, the wave, and the sparkles. If it's also in the callout blocks AND the background AND the border, it loses its punch.
- **Drop shadow missing.** Any product that shares tonal value with the background will disappear without it.
- **Two-tone header skipped.** If the header is one color, it's not a DFO image. This rule has no exceptions at size 194.
