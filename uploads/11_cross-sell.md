# Template 11 — Cross Sell

**Reference Image:** `11_Cross_Sell.png`
**Carousel Slot:** Typically the last image in the carousel — Image 8 or 9. After the customer is sold on the current product, this image reminds them the brand has a full ecosystem.

---

## What This Image Must Do

Cross Sell does two things simultaneously: it deepens the purchase occasion and it extends the brand relationship. A customer who just decided to buy the gel heel socks is a customer who might also buy the arch support sleeves. A customer buying one compression glove SKU might want to see the full glove family before they decide on style.

But the Cross Sell template isn't just a product dump. It has a framing angle — a headline that creates a context for the multi-product display. That context is usually one of three things: gifting occasion ("GIFTABLE SELF CARE"), product family breadth ("DESIGNED BY DOCTORS / Optimized for each occasion"), or a lifestyle angle that multiple products serve ("Complete your self-care routine").

The products shown must have a clear visual hierarchy. The "hero" product (the one the customer is already looking at) gets prominent treatment. Supporting products are arrayed below or beside it, clearly labeled and differentiated. The customer should be able to scan this image and understand: "there are multiple products here, they're all by the same trusted brand, and I might want more than one."

## Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | Scaled to layout | Two-tone format. Sets the framing angle — gifting, family, occasion, etc. |
| Product Labels | Poppins SemiBold | Clear, legible at thumbnail | Each product in the layout gets a distinct label beneath its image. Short — the product name or a descriptor. |
| CTA / Sub | Poppins Medium | Supportive | Beneath the header — adds context or a specific offer. E.g., "Giftable Packaging to Treat Yourself (or a loved one)." |

### Header Two-Tone Rule
Applies at the standard threshold. In the reference image: "GIFTABLE" (dark neutral) + "SELF CARE" (product accent). Or: "DESIGNED" (dark neutral) + "BY DOCTORS" (accent). The header must immediately frame why multiple products are being shown. The context comes first (dark neutral), the benefit or angle second (accent).

## Layout Variants

### Variant 1 — Gift / Occasion Framing

**Structure:**
- Header at top: gifting angle headline — "GIFTABLE SELF CARE" or "THE GIFT THAT HEALS."
- Sub copy beneath header: 1–2 lines of supporting context — "Giftable Packaging to Treat Yourself (or a loved one)."
- Hero product image: large, prominently placed. Often with gift-context visual cues — a gift bag, a bow, festive packaging.
- Milestone callout badge: if the product has a sales milestone, it appears here — a small pill/badge with "1.8 Million Socks Sold" in Poppins SemiBold. The milestone badge gives the gifting context a social proof dimension.
- Media logo bar: if applicable, 2–3 recognizable media logos appear beneath the badge. This is the Social Proof element in abbreviated form.
- Product variant strip: at the bottom, a horizontal row of the product in all available colorways/variants, each labeled. Accompanied by footer text — "Available in 7 stunning colors" or "Available in [X] colors."
- Optional: "Gift wrapping available at checkout. Gift receipt available." — functional copy at a small size.

**When to use this variant:**
- Holiday season
- Any product that comes in multiple colorways (socks, gloves)
- Products positioned as thoughtful gifts for the target customer's lifestyle

---

### Variant 2 — Product Family / Occasion Coverage

**Structure:**
- Header: product family claim — "DESIGNED BY DOCTORS / Optimized for each occasion."
- Prominent media badge in the upper right: "As Seen On: [Network]" — a small but visible credibility anchor at the beginning of the image.
- Product images: 3–5 products from the same family arrayed either side by side or in a slight fan/spread composition. Products are sized consistently — this variant doesn't have a single hero product, it presents the family as equals.
- Product labels beneath each: short product names or descriptor (Original Arthritis Gloves, Grippy Arthritis Gloves, Copper Compression Gloves, Fullfinger Copper Compression Gloves).
- Footer: "Available in Multiple Styles & Sizes" — a single footer line that ties the array together.

**When to use this variant:**
- Products that come in 3+ related SKUs (glove family, sock family, bandage variety)
- When the customer benefit is choosing the right variant for their specific occasion or need
- When breadth is the differentiator — "we have a version for everyone"

## Design Elements Required

| Element | Placement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hero Product Image | Prominent — upper center or upper left | Largest product image in the layout. Clear, clean. |
| Supporting Product Images | Below or beside hero | Smaller, equally lit. Each gets a label. |
| Product Label Text | Beneath each product image | Poppins SemiBold. Short. Product name or descriptor. |
| Milestone Badge | Near hero product | Pill/badge shape in product accent. "X Million Sold" or equivalent. |
| Media Logo Bar (abbreviated) | Near badge or below sub copy | 2–3 logos maximum in Cross Sell. Full logo bar is the Social Proof template's job. |
| Colorway Swatch Strip | Bottom of canvas (Gifting variant) | Product in all colorways, labeled. Horizontal row. |
| Media Credibility Badge | Upper corner (Product Family variant) | "As Seen On:" + media name in a small pill badge. |
| Authority Signature (Optional) | Can appear if doctor identity is the primary hook | Handwritten signature + credentials. Uses the same rules as Template 08. |
| Wave / Gentle Curve Shape | Background divider | Standard accent color. Separates header zone from product display zone. |

## Color Guidance

- **Background:** The color palette shifts to match the occasion or product family. For gifting variants, a warmer, richer accent (purple, maroon) has been used in the reference to create a premium gift feel — even if that color is outside the standard DFO palette for that SKU. For product family variants, a neutral background (white, light) lets the product array breathe.
- **Header:** Standard two-tone — dark neutral base + product accent emphasis.
- **Milestone badge:** Product accent fill. White text. High contrast.
- **Product labels:** Dark neutral. Legible against the background. No colorization.
- **Colorway swatches:** Actual product color hex values. Each swatch separated by a gap or thin divider.
- **Media badge:** Small — credibility signal, not the focal point. Navy or teal fill with white text is standard.

## What Good Looks Like

In `11_Cross_Sell.png`, two clear variants:

Left variant (Gifting): "GIFTABLE SELF CARE" header in a deep purple/maroon tone — the product accent for this heel sock SKU. Sub copy explains the occasion. A gift bag wrapping the product is the hero image. The milestone badge "1.8 Million Socks Sold" appears in a dark oval. Media logos (ELLE, People) sit below. A 7-color variant strip runs along the bottom with all colorways labeled. "Available in 7 stunning colors" footer.

Right variant (Product Family): "DESIGNED BY DOCTORS / Optimized for each occasion" header on a blue background. "As Seen On: AMC NETWORKS" badge in the upper right. Four compression glove variants spread across the bottom half — Original Arthritis Gloves, Grippy Arthritis Gloves, Copper Compression Gloves, Fullfinger Copper Compression Gloves — each labeled, each shown at equal scale. "Available in Multiple Styles & Sizes" footer.

Both variants demonstrate the core principle: multiple products, clear labels, one unifying frame.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Products without labels.** If a customer can't tell which product is which at a glance, the cross-sell fails. Every product needs a clear label.
- **Too many products.** Five is the maximum before the image becomes a catalog page. For anything beyond 5 variants, group or abstract them.
- **Hero product not distinguished.** In the gifting variant, one product is the hook — give it size and prominence. In the family variant, all products are equal — keep them equal.
- **Missing milestone badge.** If the product has a sales milestone (1 million+ units, #1 bestseller), this is the template where it belongs — it validates the cross-sell by proving existing demand.
- **No framing headline.** A collection of product photos without a headline is a catalog, not an image. The header creates the narrative that makes the cross-sell purposeful.
- **Colorway strip too small to read.** The swatch strip at the bottom should be large enough to distinguish the colors clearly at mobile size. If the colors are similar, add labels beneath each swatch.
- **Authority signature when not needed.** Only use the doctor signature in Cross Sell if physician-designed is the primary differentiator being claimed. If the cross-sell is occasion-based (gifting) or variety-based, the signature competes with the product display.
