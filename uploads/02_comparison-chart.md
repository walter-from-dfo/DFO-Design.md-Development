# Template 02 — Comparison Chart

**Reference Image:** `02_Comparison_Chart.png`
**Carousel Slot:** Typically Image 3 or 4. After the customer has been hooked by the hero and offer images, this image answers the question: "Why this brand over the others?"

---

## What This Image Must Do

The Comparison Chart template is a visual argument. It makes the case — quickly and visually — that Dr. Frederick's Original outperforms generic alternatives on every dimension that matters. The structure is a grid: rows are features, columns are products. But the visual weight is anything but equal. The brand column dominates. The competitor columns recede. By the time the customer's eye reaches the bottom of the grid, the conclusion is foregone.

This image doesn't need to be subtle. It needs to be clear and decisive. The customer scanning this at thumbnail size should see one thing: the brand column is full of checkmarks, and the competition isn't.

---

## Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 194 | Two-tone color format. The header anchors the entire image and sets the competitive framing — e.g., "DESIGN THAT'S STEPS AHEAD". |
| Brand Title | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 42 | Column header for the brand product. Larger visual weight than competitor titles. |
| Competitor Title | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 42 | Column headers for competitor products. Same font as brand title but visually subordinate via color/size treatment. |
| Main Sub | Poppins SemiBold | 36 | Feature row labels — the name of each feature being compared. |
| Secondary Sub | Poppins Regular | 24 | Supporting detail within a row — smaller descriptor or clarifying note beneath the main sub. |
| Body | Poppins Medium | 50 | Larger supporting copy if used for a tagline panel or callout section below the grid. |
| Disclaimer | Poppins Regular | 24 | Fine print at the bottom — comparison methodology note, asterisk references. Always include. |

### Header Two-Tone Rule
At size 194, the header uses the two-tone split. The framing phrase ("DESIGN THAT'S") sits in dark neutral; the knockout phrase ("STEPS AHEAD") hits in the product accent color. The header must name the competitive advantage conceptually — it's a brand positioning statement, not just a label.

---

## Layout Structure

The canvas divides into a top header zone and a main grid zone.

**Top Header Zone**
- Header sits at the top left or top center in large Neuzeit type.
- 3-Sparkle brand graphic (2-color) appears near the header — typically upper right of the text block or floating above the brand column.

**Main Grid Zone**
- Columns: brand product column (left or center, always dominant) + 1–3 competitor columns (right, receding).
- The brand column gets a saturated color fill — the product accent color or deep navy. It's wider, taller, and bolder than the competitor columns.
- The competitor columns use muted gray tones. If competitor products have images, they are smaller. The visual message: those are generic; this is the real thing.
- Each row represents one feature. The feature name sits in the leftmost column as the row label (Main Sub size). Each column then shows a filled checkmark (brand has it) or an X / empty circle (competitor doesn't).
- **Checkmark style:** Filled circle with a white checkmark inside for the brand. Empty circle or red X for competitors. The filled circle uses the brand accent color — it's the same visual energy as the header accent word.
- The brand column's product image is placed at the top of the column, large and prominent. Competitor images are smaller and positioned similarly but with less visual emphasis.

**Footer / Tagline Panel (Optional)**
- Below the grid, a single-line tagline or positioning statement can appear.
- At the very bottom: the disclaimer in Poppins Regular 24. This is a legal necessity — the comparison is to "traditional bandages" or "other brands" generally, not named competitors.

**Multi-Product Variant**
The reference image shows a version that compares across multiple DFO product SKUs (e.g., Fuzzy Socks vs. Original Socks vs. Other Brands). In this variant, each DFO product gets its own column, all with saturated treatment, and competitor columns still recede. The brand wins on every column — it's a product family showcase as much as a comparison.

## Design Elements Required

| Element | Placement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 3 Sparkles (2-color) | Near the brand column header or upper right of the header text | Reinforces brand identity at the top of the comparison. |
| Filled Checkmark Icons | Brand column rows | White check inside a filled circle in the product accent color. |
| Empty or X Icons | Competitor column rows | Gray circle outline or red X. Keep consistent within the image. |
| Brand Product Image | Top of brand column | Larger than competitor images. Clean cutout preferred. |
| Competitor Product Images | Top of competitor columns | Smaller, grayed-back or desaturated to visually recede. |
| Disclaimer Text | Bottom of image | Always present. References comparison methodology. |

---

## Color Guidance

- **Brand column background:** Product accent color or deep navy (`#1d396a`). Fully saturated, confident.
- **Competitor column background:** Light gray or near-white. Muted. No saturation.
- **Header base word:** Near-black or deep navy.
- **Header accent word:** Product accent color.
- **Checkmarks:** White icon inside a filled circle using product accent color.
- **X or empty icons:** Gray or muted red. Never bright or attention-grabbing.
- **Row dividers:** Subtle — light gray lines or slightly alternating tint rows. They guide the eye without adding visual noise.

## What Good Looks Like

Looking at `02_Comparison_Chart.png`, the first variant (left) shows a narrow-column layout with DFO bandages vs. Regular Bandages vs. Other Brands. The DFO column is wider and sits at the center, pulling all visual weight. The header "DESIGN THAT'S STEPS AHEAD" frames the comparison before you even read a row. The second variant (center) shows the compression gloves comparison against a "Copycat" — teal color treatment, large product image in the DFO column, and the competitor column has a literal "COPYCAT" label that does real rhetorical work. The third variant (right) compares four DFO sock products — all get saturated treatment because this is a product family showcase, not a vs-competitor chart.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Brand and competitor columns equal in visual weight.** The brand must win visually before you even read the rows.
- **Too many rows.** 6–8 features is the sweet spot. More than 10 and the grid becomes unreadable at mobile size.
- **Named competitors.** Never name real brands in this comparison — use "Regular Bandages," "Other Brands," "Copycat." Legal risk otherwise.
- **No disclaimer.** Always include the fine print at the bottom. It provides legal cover for the comparison claims.
- **Sparkles missing.** This template requires the 3-sparkle brand graphic in the brand column area.
- **Checkmarks in a plain font.** The checkmarks should be icon-style — filled circles with white checks — not typographic characters.
