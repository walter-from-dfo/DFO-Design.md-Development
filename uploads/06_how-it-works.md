# Template 06 — How It Works / How To Use

**Reference Image:** `06_How_It_Works_or_How_To_Use.png`
**Carousel Slot:** Typically Image 5 or 6. Sits late in the carousel — by now the customer is close to buying and just needs to know the product is easy to use.

---

## What This Image Must Do

How It Works removes the last barrier: "I don't know how to use this correctly." Whether it's applying a finger splint, putting on gel socks, or layering a bandage — confusion kills conversions. This template takes the application process and breaks it into numbered steps that are clear, visual, and achievable.

The tone here is instructional but not clinical. Each step should feel like a helpful friend walking you through something, not a medical manual. The step numbers are huge — size 120 — because the customer needs to scan them immediately and understand: this is 4 steps, not 40.

There are two named variants in this template: **"HOW IT WORKS"** (explains the product's mechanism — what it does inside) and **"HOW TO USE"** / **"HOW TO WEAR"** (explains the application — what you do to put it on). The layout spec is shared. The content framing is different.

## Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 180–200 | Two-tone color format. The range (180–200) allows for headline length variation. |
| Sub (Regular) | Poppins Regular | 64 | Supporting subtitle beneath the header — the "what this image explains" line. |
| Sub (SemiBold) | Poppins SemiBold | 64 | Emphasized portion of the subtitle. Used in tandem with the Regular weight. |
| Detail Number | Poppins SemiBold | 120 | Step number. Dominant. Should be the largest element in each step panel. |
| Step Body (Medium) | Poppins Medium | 67 | The instruction text for each step. Conversational, action-led language. |
| Step Body (SemiBold) | Poppins SemiBold | 67 | Emphasized words within the step body — key actions or key product terms. |
| Footer / Caption | Poppins SemiBold | 50 | Optional footer line at the bottom of the canvas — e.g., "Dr. Frederick's Instructions For Happy Heels Included!" |
| Fine Body | Poppins Regular | 50 | Supporting text at the footer level — secondary note, URL, or fine print. |

### Header Two-Tone Rule
At 180–200, the header must use two-tone. The head in the reference image reads "HOW IT WORKS" with the first phrase in dark neutral and the specific detail (e.g., "HexFlex™ Moves With You") in the accent color as the sub. In other variants, the header itself splits: "HOW IT" in dark neutral, "WORKS" or "TO WEAR" in accent. The rule is consistent — dark neutral anchors, accent color lands the punch.

### The Sub Pair Rule
The sub pair here mirrors the Product Details template logic: one line in Poppins Regular 64, the adjacent or next line in Poppins SemiBold 64. The Regular introduces context; the SemiBold names the specific claim. Together they form a single thought with natural emphasis.

## Layout Structure

**Top Zone — Header + Sub**
- Large Neuzeit header, two-tone.
- Sub pair beneath it — Regular then SemiBold (or vice versa depending on which word is being emphasized).
- The wave shape element runs at the top of this zone as a background band, or at the bottom as a ground element.

**Step Panels — Grid or Horizontal Row**
This is the core of the template. Steps are arranged in panels — either:
- **2×2 grid** (4 steps total, most common)
- **Horizontal row** (2–3 steps)
- **Vertical column** with photos and instructions side by side

Each step panel contains:
1. **Step Number** — Poppins SemiBold 120. Huge. It's the first thing the eye catches in each panel.
2. **Step Photo** — An in-use photo showing exactly what happens in this step. Hands, product, body zone in a clear, well-lit close-up.
3. **Step Body** — The instruction text. Action-led: "Place your finger on top of the splint..." "Wrap the bottom strap around the base..." Conversational. Specific. Short.

**Color Variation Across Panels**
Each step panel uses a slightly different background tint — not identical solid fills. The variation is subtle (a 10–20% lightness shift or a slight hue rotation within the same accent family) but it creates visual rhythm and helps the customer differentiate step 1 from step 2 from step 3 from step 4. This is not random — it's a deliberate pacing device.

**Connecting Arrows**
Between panels, directional arrows point from step to next step. These arrows are **the same color as the accent word in the header**. This is a design system rule: the arrows inherit their color from the header accent, tying the navigation color to the brand identity color for that product.

**Gentle Curve Shape**
In some variants, a gentle curve shape (softer than the wave — more of a swooping arc) appears at the bottom of the step grid as a grounding element. It's the same accent color as the wave but at lower visual intensity.

**Footer**
An optional footer line at the bottom — Poppins SemiBold 50 — can add a closing encouragement or reference to included instructions.

## The "HOW TO WEAR" Variant

The "How To Wear" variant (seen in the right panel of `06_How_It_Works_or_How_To_Use.png`) replaces numbered steps with numbered circle icons. Instead of a full step panel, each instruction gets:
- A numbered circle (the number inside a filled circle in the accent color)
- A short action instruction (Poppins Medium or SemiBold)
- A supporting photo beside it

This variant works well for products with very simple 2-step application (e.g., "Slide onto middle three toes" + "Wear under snug socks"). It's faster to produce and reads more like a quick reference than a tutorial.

## Design Elements Required

| Element | Placement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Wave Shape | Top band or bottom band of the header zone | Accent color. Sets the background tone for the header. |
| Gentle Curve Shape | Bottom of the step grid, beneath the panels | Softer, lower intensity than the wave. |
| Connecting Arrows | Between step panels | Accent color — same hex as header emphasis word. Direction: left to right, or top to bottom depending on grid orientation. |
| Step Number (120pt) | Top-left or top-center of each step panel | Dominant. Larger than the step photo caption. |
| Step Photos | Inside each step panel | In-use, hands-on, specific to the action described. |

---

## Color Guidance

- **Background:** Dark navy (`#1d396a`) for a premium, authoritative feel — or a very light tint of the accent for a warmer approachable feel. The reference image shows both a navy-background and a cream-background variant.
- **Header text on dark background:** Cream (`#fef5e3`) or white for base words; accent color for emphasis words.
- **Step panel backgrounds:** Slight tonal variations. If the overall image is dark, panels can be slightly varying shades of navy/dark teal. If light, panels are off-white with slight accent tinting.
- **Step numbers:** Accent color or cream, depending on panel background. Always the highest contrast element in the panel.
- **Connecting arrows:** Exact hex of the header accent word. Non-negotiable.
- **Gentle curve:** Same accent palette, lower opacity or lighter shade.

## What Good Looks Like

In `06_How_It_Works_or_How_To_Use.png`, the left variant shows a "HOW IT WORKS / HexFlex™ Moves With You" layout with a light cream background, wave shape above, and a horizontal banner of feature callouts below the header. This variant is more product-explanation than step-by-step — it annotates the product's material properties rather than showing application steps.

The center variant shows the 4-panel 2×2 grid with step photos for applying a finger splint. Each panel has the step number (120pt) dominant, a photo showing the hand action, and brief instruction text. The gentle curve shape grounds the bottom. Arrows move the eye from step 1 to step 2 to step 3 to step 4.

The right variant shows the simplified "HOW TO WEAR" version — 2 steps, numbered circles, photos beside each.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Steps numbered with small text.** If the step number isn't visually dominant at 120pt, the customer doesn't instantly know how many steps there are. Scale up.
- **Arrows in a neutral color.** Gray arrows are invisible. The arrow must be the accent color — it's a navigation element, not decoration.
- **Step panels with identical background fills.** The subtle variation is what creates rhythm. Identical fills make the grid look like a table.
- **Step body text too long.** Each step instruction should be 1–2 sentences maximum. If it's 3 sentences, cut it.
- **Header in one color.** Two-tone at 180–200 is mandatory. No exceptions.
- **No footer.** The footer often carries a small emotional close ("Instructions included!" "Works in just 2 steps."). Without it, the image ends abruptly.
