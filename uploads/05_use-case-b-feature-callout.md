# Template 05 — Use Case B (Single Item / Feature Callout)

**Reference Image:** `05_Use_Case_2.png`
**Carousel Slot:** Typically Image 4 or 5. Sits in the informational middle of the carousel — after desire is established, before the close.

---

## What This Image Must Do

Use Case B puts a single product front and center and uses visual annotation — a pop-out circle, badges, and direct callouts — to communicate what makes it special. Where Use Case A shows context and placement across multiple scenarios, Use Case B zooms in. One product. One moment of use. Multiple reasons it was designed exactly right.

This is the "prove it" template. The customer has seen the product and the claims — now they want evidence. The pop-out circle detail is the key device: it crops into a specific zone of the product (the grip texture, the gel lining, the compression weave) and magnifies it so the customer can see the material quality for themselves. Alongside it, icon badges compress each benefit into a scannable signal.

The result should feel like a product review written in visual language — specific, credible, and worth pausing for.

---

## Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 140–142 | Two-tone color format. Uses a two-phrase structure — an action paired with a result, or a problem paired with a solution. |
| Sub | Poppins Medium | 56 | One-line supporting sentence beneath the header. Expands on the header claim. |
| Features | Poppins SemiBold | 50 | Labels for each badge. Short — 2–4 words. Each badge label is one scannable benefit. |

### Header Two-Tone Rule
At 140, two-tone applies. The distinctive pattern here is the **two-phrase header format**: "MOVE FREELY," (dark neutral) + "HOLD STEADY" (accent color). The first phrase names the state or problem; the second names the outcome. The comma or line break between them creates rhythm. Both phrases are short, punchy, and oppositional in a satisfying way.

---

## Layout Structure

This template is vertically oriented with two main zones — the left/center product zone and the right annotation zone.

**Product Zone (Left / Center)**
- The hero product image occupies most of the visual field. It's in-use — on a body part, in a scene — not a studio cutout.
- The product should be photographed at a slight angle or from a perspective that reveals its texture and construction.
- The wave shape (and sometimes the gentle curve shape) sits at the bottom of this zone, grounding the product and adding a background layer of brand identity color.
- The tab feature element appears near the top of this zone — a small tab or flag that labels the product line, SKU, or a key attribute.

**Pop-Out Circle Detail**
- A circular crop of the product's most compelling material or structural detail is placed floating near the product — connected to it by an arrow.
- The circle has a thick, high-contrast outline. The outline color is the same as the product accent. It creates visual pop — the circle reads as a deliberate annotation, not a random crop.
- The arrow connecting the circle to the product source point is drawn in the **same color as the circle outline**. This matching creates a visual system: accent color = attention, annotation, proof.
- The crop inside the circle should show something worth zooming into: grip dots, gel material, compression weave, moisture-wicking texture. It's evidence of quality.

**Annotation / Badge Zone (Right)**
- A vertical stack of badges sits along the right edge of the canvas, beside the product image.
- Each badge is a small rounded icon shape — a white symbol on a colored background. The background color is complementary to the image's hero accent, not identical.
- The badge symbol (icon) represents the benefit wordlessly: anti-slip dots icon, hand tremor waves icon, grip lines icon, heart icon, temperature icon, hypoallergenic leaf icon.
- Beneath each badge, the Feature label (Poppins SemiBold 50) names the benefit in 2–4 words.
- Badges are arranged in a clean vertical list with generous spacing. They are scannable from top to bottom in under 3 seconds.

## Design Elements Required

| Element | Placement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pop-Out Circle Detail | Floating beside or above the product | Thick accent-color outline. Inside: magnified material detail. |
| Arrow from Product to Circle | Connecting product to pop-out circle | Same color as circle outline. Accent color. |
| Badge Icons (vertical stack) | Right side of canvas | White icon on accent-complementary colored background. 4–6 badges typical. |
| Wave Shape | Bottom of canvas / bottom of product zone | Accent color. Grounds the layout. |
| Gentle Curve Shape | Optionally at base, subtler than wave | If using, it sits below the wave as a softer grounding element. |
| Tab Feature Element | Top of product zone | Small, directional label for product line or key feature. |

## Color Guidance

- **Background:** White or very light background tint. The product and badges carry all the color weight — the background should not compete.
- **Header base phrase:** Near-black (`#231f20`) or deep navy.
- **Header accent phrase:** Product line accent color.
- **Pop-out circle outline:** Product accent color — must be high contrast against both the background and the product image.
- **Arrow:** Exact same hex as the circle outline. Do not use a lighter or darker variant.
- **Badge backgrounds:** Complementary to the accent — if the accent is teal, badges can be a darker teal or a warm complementary. Never the exact same color as the circle outline (they need to feel like a system, not a monotone).
- **Badge symbols:** White. Always white on color. No exceptions.
- **Wave shape:** Accent color — mid-saturation.

## What Good Looks Like

In `05_Use_Case_2.png`, the left variant shows compression gloves in an in-use moment (cutting vegetables) with a pop-out circle zooming into the grip dot texture. The circle outline is a warm complement to the glove fabric. The arrow points directly to the finger grip zone on the product. The right variant adds the wave shape at bottom and shows 5 badges in a vertical stack: Anti-Slip Dots, Helps with Hand Tremors, Tighter Grip & Control, Superior Durability, Provides Cozy Heat, Hypoallergenic. Each badge is a circle with a white symbol. The Feature labels beside each badge (Poppins SemiBold 50) are short and specific.

The header "FROM SLIPPERY / TO STEADY" uses the two-phrase format: problem phrase + solution phrase.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Pop-out circle without an arrow.** Without the connecting arrow, the circle feels like a random crop. The arrow is essential — it explains the relationship.
- **Arrow in a different color than the circle.** They are the same element in the design system. They must match.
- **Too many badges.** 4–6 is the range. Beyond 6, each badge loses its distinctiveness and the stack becomes a list.
- **Badge icons too complex.** The symbol inside each badge should read at small sizes. Simple line icons, not detailed illustrations.
- **Header in one color.** Two-phrase, two-tone is the signature of this template. One color = generic ecommerce.
- **Product too small.** The product image should occupy at least 50% of the canvas width. This is a feature callout — the product is the thing being featured.
- **Wave shape missing.** Without it, the bottom of the canvas is empty and ungrounded.
