# Template 07 — Sizing Chart

**Reference Image:** `07_Sizing_Chart.png`
**Carousel Slot:** Typically Image 6 or 7. A necessary functional image — placed after the persuasive images because it answers "does this fit me?" rather than "do I want this?"

---

## What This Image Must Do

The Sizing Chart eliminates the most common reason for returns: wrong size. It gives the customer a clear, visual, actionable way to determine which size to order before they buy. This image should be so clear that a customer can look at it, grab a ruler, and know their size in under a minute.

But this is not just a size table. It's still a DFO brand image. The premium aesthetic must survive the functional content. A size chart that looks like a spreadsheet is a missed opportunity — it breaks the brand feeling right when the customer is closest to converting. Done correctly, this template is beautiful and useful simultaneously.

The key devices: a large, scannable grid with subtle tonal variation per row, in-use photography showing how to measure, and badges communicating care and fit attributes in scannable icon form.

## Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Header | Neuzeit Grotesk ExtCond Black | 200 | Two-tone format. At this size it can only be 2–3 words. Sets the tone before the table. |
| Sub (Medium) | Poppins Medium | 67 | First line of subtitle. Context sentence — e.g., "Measure across the palm to match your size on the chart." |
| Sub (SemiBold) | Poppins SemiBold | 67 | Emphasized sub line. Works in tandem with the Medium line — same size, heavier weight for the key claim or instruction. |
| Measurement | Poppins Medium | 52 | The dimension values inside the sizing grid — e.g., "2¼–2⅝in", "3¼–4in". These must be highly legible. |
| Features (SemiBold) | Poppins SemiBold | 50 | Feature lines outside the grid — brief attribute callouts (e.g., "Expect gentle shedding as a result of cozy construction"). |
| Features (Regular) | Poppins Regular | 46 | Lighter-weight line adjacent to the SemiBold feature. The two weights together create a reading rhythm. |

### Header Two-Tone Rule
At 200, the header is large enough to demand the two-tone treatment. The reference examples show "FIND YOUR FIT" (dark neutral + accent) and "LUXURIOUSLY SOFT & FUZZY" (dark neutral + accent). The tone here can be more product-feeling ("Luxuriously") because the customer is already interested — they're looking for confirmation and sizing help.

### The Two-Weight Feature Pair
Unique to the Sizing Chart is the deliberate pairing of Poppins Regular 46 and Poppins SemiBold 50 in adjacent feature lines. The Regular line introduces context; the SemiBold line delivers the specific attribute. Together they form a rhythm — the eye moves naturally from lighter to heavier. When a feature runs to two lines, both weights are used: the first line lighter, the second heavier. This keeps even dense copy from feeling like a wall of text.

## Layout Structure

**Top Zone — Header + Sub + 3 Sparkles**
- Large Neuzeit header at top. Two-tone.
- Sub pair beneath — Medium then SemiBold (or vice versa).
- The 3-sparkle brand graphic (2-color) is prominently placed — typically upper right or adjacent to the header. Support color for the sparkles should match the product fabric or material color (e.g., if the sock is pink, the sparkle support color echoes that pink).

**Grid Zone — Sizing Table**
- The sizing table occupies the center-lower portion of the canvas.
- Columns: Size label (S, M, L, XL) + Measurement range (2¼–2⅝in, etc.).
- The current recommended or highlighted size row uses the product accent color as a fill — it's the "this is probably you" visual cue.
- Other rows use very light tints — each row is a slightly different shade, not identical fills. The variation creates grid readability without using visible dividers.
- The highlighted row can use a slightly larger font weight or a subtle pill/label behind the size name.

**Measurement Photo Zone**
- An in-use photo shows how to measure. For gloves: a hand with a measuring tape across the palm. For socks: a foot beside a ruler. For sleeves: the relevant body zone with measurement landmarks.
- Measurement lines are overlaid on the photo with leader lines pointing to specific anatomical landmarks — Poppins Medium 52. These are clinical but clean.
- The photo is cropped tightly. This is not a lifestyle shot — it's a measurement reference. The body part and measuring tool are the whole story.

**Feature Callouts (Right or Below Grid)**
- 2–4 feature lines in the two-weight pairing system (SemiBold + Regular).
- These reference product attributes that relate to fit and comfort: "One Size Fits Most", "Stays in Place, Stays Comfy", "Hand wash & air dry".
- Optional: badges for care instructions. Same badge system as Use Case B — white symbol on a complementary-color shape. Care badges (hand wash icon, air dry icon, machine wash icon) are common here.

## Color Guidance

- **Background:** White or very light. The grid needs to breathe — a colored background competes with the row tint variations.
- **Grid highlighted row:** Product accent color fill. The one row that says "this is your size."
- **Grid other rows:** Very light tints — near-white with subtle shifts. Each row a slightly different lightness level.
- **Header base word:** Near-black or deep navy.
- **Header accent word:** Product accent color.
- **Measurement text:** Dark neutral. High legibility is the priority. Do not colorize measurements.
- **Feature lines (SemiBold):** Dark neutral or product accent, depending on emphasis needed.
- **Feature lines (Regular):** Medium gray or dark neutral at lower weight.
- **Sparkles:** Primary sparkle in product accent; support sparkle in the color matching the product's physical material.

In `07_Sizing_Chart.png`, the left variant shows the glove sizing chart — "FIND YOUR FIT" header in two-tone, a hand with a measuring tape photo, and a 4-row grid (Small through Extra Large) with the Medium row highlighted in teal. The right variant shows the fuzzy sock sizing — "LUXURIOUSLY SOFT & FUZZY" header, 3-sparkle graphic with a support color matching the pink sock fabric, care badges (hand wash, air dry), and feature copy in the two-weight pairing system.

Both variants prove the rule: size data + premium brand aesthetics can coexist when the grid is treated with the same care as the rest of the image.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Grid with identical row fills.** The tonal variation is what makes the grid readable without heavy borders. Identical fills create a flat wall of data.
- **No highlighted row.** The customer needs a visual signal for the "most common" or featured size. Without it, the grid has no anchor.
- **Measurement photo missing.** Telling someone to measure is not enough. Show them how. The photo turns instruction into demonstration.
- **Sparkle support color mismatched.** The support color of the sparkle should echo the product's material color. A pink sock product gets a pink sparkle support. A gray glove product gets a gray sparkle support. This is a design detail that ties the graphic to the product.
- **Feature lines all the same weight.** The SemiBold / Regular alternation is what creates rhythm. All SemiBold reads as urgent; all Regular reads as boring. The pairing reads as designed.
- **Header too long.** At 200pt, 2–3 words is the maximum before it wraps awkwardly. "FIND YOUR FIT" is perfect. "FIND YOUR PERFECT FIT TODAY" is too long.
